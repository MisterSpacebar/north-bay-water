========================================================================
FINDINGS: Bay Platform <-> Underwater Mission Correlation
========================================================================

DATA COVERAGE
----------------------------------------
* Platform data: 2025-03 through 2025-12 (7 stations, L0-L6)
* Missions: Mar 15 2024 (dry), Oct 25 2024 (wet), Mar 18 2025 (dry)
* Only March 2025 missions have direct temporal overlap with platforms
* 2024 comparisons are structural (same parameter, different location/time)

1. SPATIAL GRADIENT -- INLAND TO BARRIER ISLANDS
----------------------------------------
  Canal/River (L1,L3,L4):  DO = 4.50 +/- 2.26 mg/L  |  Salinity = 4.0 PPT
  Open Bay (L0,L2):        DO = 5.95 +/- 1.70 mg/L  |  Salinity = 21.7 PPT
  Causeway (L5,L6):        DO = 6.26 +/- 1.20 mg/L  |  Salinity = 23.3 PPT
  Barrier Islands (Mar 2024 (dry)): DO = 6.84 +/- 0.48 mg/L  |  Salinity = 33.1 PPT
  Barrier Islands (Mar 2025 (dry)): DO = 6.86 +/- 0.19 mg/L  |  Salinity = 33.2 PPT
  Barrier Islands (Oct 2024 (wet)): DO = 7.36 +/- 1.46 mg/L  |  Salinity = 30.9 PPT

  -> Key finding: Water quality improves dramatically from inland
    freshwater sources toward the barrier islands. The missions
    near the holocene barriers consistently show healthy DO (>6 mg/L)
    while canal/river stations (L1, L3) are chronically hypoxic.
    Canal/river hypoxic readings: 15.6%
    Barrier-island mission hypoxic readings: 0.0%

2. SEASONAL EFFECTS (same barrier-island location)
----------------------------------------
  Mar 2024 (dry):
    DO:          6.84 +/- 0.48 mg/L  (range 4.3-8.2)
    Salinity:    33.1 +/- 5.3 PPT  (range 0.2-36.1)
    Temperature: 25.4 +/- 0.4 deg C
    Turbidity:   median 8.4 FNU  (mean 113.9, skewed by spikes up to 3420)
  Mar 2025 (dry):
    DO:          6.86 +/- 0.19 mg/L  (range 6.6-7.8)
    Salinity:    33.2 +/- 4.1 PPT  (range 4.2-35.0)
    Temperature: 24.0 +/- 0.2 deg C
    Turbidity:   median 2.2 FNU  (mean 21.6, skewed by spikes up to 1736)
  Oct 2024 (wet):
    DO:          7.36 +/- 1.46 mg/L  (range 2.7-13.1)
    Salinity:    30.9 +/- 4.1 PPT  (range 0.2-34.9)
    Temperature: 27.6 +/- 0.3 deg C
    Turbidity:   median 12.3 FNU  (mean 133.5, skewed by spikes up to 2130)

  -> Dry season (March) vs Wet season (October):
    Temperature: 24.6deg C (dry) vs 27.6deg C (wet)  -- +2.9deg C warmer
    Salinity: 33.1 PPT (dry) vs 30.9 PPT (wet)  -- lower in wet season
    DO: 6.84 mg/L (dry) vs 7.36 mg/L (wet)
    Warmer wet-season water holds less DO (solubility effect),
    but missions still show healthy levels at barrier islands.
    Possible freshwater runoff influence visible in lower salinity readings.

3. WATER MASS IDENTIFICATION (T-S ANALYSIS)
----------------------------------------
  Canal/river stations (L1, L3): nearly fresh (<1-8 PPT), wide T range
  Open bay (L0, L2): brackish-marine mix (16-24 PPT)
  Causeway (L5, L6): marine-dominated (19-33 PPT)
  Barrier-island missions: strongly marine (30-35 PPT in dry season)

  -> The missions sample water closer to open-ocean influence
    than any of the fixed platforms. L6 (south of causeway)
    is the platform closest in salinity to mission readings.
    This confirms missions are in a distinct, more oceanic
    water mass separated from canal/river discharge.

4. WHAT MISSIONS REVEAL THAT PLATFORMS CANNOT
----------------------------------------
  * Vertical water-column structure (depth profiles of DO, salinity,
    turbidity) -- platforms are fixed at one depth
  * High-frequency spatial transects (GPS-tracked)
  * Chlorophyll/phycocyanin (algal bloom indicators, March 2024)
  * pH measurements (ocean acidification context, March 2024)
  * Conditions at the barrier islands themselves -- no platform exists there

5. WHAT PLATFORMS REVEAL THAT MISSIONS CANNOT
----------------------------------------
  * Long-term temporal trends and seasonal cycles
  * Diurnal (day/night) DO patterns driven by photosynthesis/respiration
  * Chronic hypoxia documentation (L3: 78% problematic, L1: 59%)
  * Freshwater discharge timing and duration
  * Multi-month context for interpreting single-day mission snapshots

6. TURBIDITY PATTERNS
----------------------------------------
  Mission median turbidity: 2.8 FNU  (generally low, with episodic spikes from bottom disturbance)
  Platform turbidity varies hugely by location:
    L1 - Biscayne Canal: median -0.1 FNU, mean 9.7 FNU
    L3 - Little River Up: median 5.6 FNU, mean 16.1 FNU
    L4 - Little River Down: median 50.4 FNU, mean 65.5 FNU
    L0 - FIU BBC: median 43.9 FNU, mean 251.8 FNU
    L2 - Canal-Bay Junction: median 0.1 FNU, mean 50.6 FNU
    L5 - NBV North: median 14.8 FNU, mean 240.9 FNU
    L6 - NBV South: median 25.1 FNU, mean 65.3 FNU
  -> High median turbidity at L5 (NBV North) and L0 (FIU BBC) suggests
    persistent sediment resuspension or runoff; barrier-island missions
    see clearer water at baseline with occasional bottom-disturbance spikes.

7. MARCH 2025 -- DIRECT TEMPORAL OVERLAP
----------------------------------------
  The only campaign with simultaneous platform data.
  Mission sonde DO: 6.86 mg/L (from 4 sonde files)
  Mission DO %sat:  76.7% (all sensors)
  Platform DO (+/-7 days of Mar 18, 2025):
    L1 - Biscayne Canal: 7.74 +/- 0.34 mg/L (14,992 readings)
    L3 - Little River Up: 4.42 +/- 0.88 mg/L (13,155 readings)
    L2 - Canal-Bay Junction: 6.26 +/- 1.88 mg/L (34,747 readings)
  -> Mission sonde readings are consistent with nearby platform L2,
    confirming the barrier-island area has comparable or better DO
    than the open-bay mixing zone.

========================================================================
CONCLUSION
========================================================================
The underwater missions near the North Bay holocene barrier islands
consistently show healthier water quality than inland platform stations:
  * Higher DO (well above stress/hypoxic thresholds)
  * Higher, more stable salinity (oceanic influence)
  * Lower baseline turbidity (with episodic bottom-disturbance spikes)
  * Temperature driven primarily by season (~25deg C dry, ~28deg C wet)

The freshwater canal/river stations (L1, L3, L4) represent a fundamentally
different water mass with chronic hypoxia and nearly zero salinity. The
gradient from these degraded inland sources to the healthy barrier-island
conditions suggests that:
  1. The barrier islands are relatively buffered from canal discharge
  2. Tidal flushing and ocean exchange maintain DO near saturation
  3. Seasonal temperature shifts affect DO solubility but not enough
     to cause stress at the barrier-island location
  4. Turbidity spikes at missions are mechanical (bottom contact)
     rather than from sustained sediment loading as seen at L0/L5

Future work: Deploy a fixed platform near the barrier islands to capture
long-term temporal patterns at that location, enabling direct time-matched
comparison with the existing network.
